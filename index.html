World COVID-19 Cases (January 2020 - July 2020)

COVID-19 pandemic is a global health crisis which has led to a dramatic loss of human life worldwide. The COVID-19 pandemic has had significant negative impact on economic including the COVID-19 recession which is the second largest global recession in recent history and 2020 stock market crash. To visualise how serioud COVID-19 is, I will show you the charts created from COVID-19(January 2020 - July 2020) datasets and its analysis.

COVID-19 Cases by Country

The map below is a choropleth map which shows the normalised total number of COVID-19 cases per 1M population of each country. The country with GREATER intensity of colour has GREATER total COVID-19 cases per 1M population. I have highlighted countries with High intensity such as CHILE,OMAN,UNITED STATES OF AMERICA,BRAZIL and PERU.

Percentage of COVID-19 Cases by Continent

The waffle chart below is showing the percentage of total number of COVID-19 cases by continent.From the waffle chart,we can clearly see that NORTH AMERICA has the GREATEST number of COVID-19 cases while AFRICA has the LEAST number of COVID-19 cases.

How Does the Total Cases Affect the Total Deaths?

The bubble chart on the right is showing how total number of COVID-19 cases affect the Total Deaths. The size is representing the population of country while the colour is representing the continent. We can see that UNITED STATES OF AMERICA has the GREATEST number of total COVID-19 cases and total deaths caused by COVID-19.

Ranking of Countries over Time

Over time,we may see that some countries may have higher number of COVID-19 cases. The bump chart on the left shows their ranking based on months from January 2020 - July 2020. We can see that US ranked NO.1 for 5 consecutive months from January 2020 to July 2020.

Trend of New Cases over Time

The area chart below shows the TREND of new COVID-19 cases from January 2020 to July 2020. We can clearly see that there was an UPWARD trend starting from March to July and the peak was at MID JULY.
